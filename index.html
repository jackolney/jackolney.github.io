About Me

I am the Executive Director of the Centre for Health Economics & Policy Innovation (CHEPI) at Imperial College Business School.

After completing a PhD in infectious disease epidemiology and mathematical modelling at Imperial College, I pivoted to a management position and moved to the Business School to support the establishment and development of CHEPI.

CHEPI is now the largest and most active research centre in the School and has established itself as the focal point for world-class research and teaching in health economics, policy, and management across Imperial College.

With no prior experience, I switched from a research career into management, which has now developed into leadership. I lead the development of our ambitious growth and impact strategies, which rely heavily on establishing new partnerships and fund raising, along with directing a team of administrative staff and overseeing all operational activities of the centre.
